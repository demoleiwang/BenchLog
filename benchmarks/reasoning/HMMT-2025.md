# HMMT 2025

> The Harvard-MIT Math Tournament problems from November 2024 + February 2025 used as an LLM benchmark — sits between AIME (saturated) and FrontierMath (research-tier). Frontier models routinely hit 90%+; well-trained open-source reasoning models reach 70–87%.

## What it measures

Mathematical problem-solving on **harder-than-AIME** competition math: difficult algebraic manipulations, non-trivial geometry, combinatorial counting, number theory, clever inequality applications. Multi-step reasoning required, often demanding pattern recognition + creative insight + careful calculation.

HMMT problems are written by college students for high-school olympiad-level talent, making them harder than AIME (which targets a broader high-school competition pool). The math style is similar to AIME (integer answers, automated grading) but the difficulty distribution is shifted higher.

The benchmark sits in a useful "twilight zone" by 2026:
- **AIME 2025** is approaching saturation (GPT-5.2 reportedly 100%)
- **HMMT 2025** is partly saturated (frontier 90%+)
- **FrontierMath Tier 4** still has frontier at ~13%

HMMT thus retains discriminative signal for non-thinking models and for sub-frontier open-source models even after AIME loses it.

## Task format

- **Input:** a problem statement from HMMT November 2024 or February 2025.
- **Output:** integer answer (HMMT problems have integer or rational closed-form answers).
- **Scoring:** accuracy across the 30-problem set. Often reported per-tournament (Nov vs. Feb) and combined.
- **Splits:** HMMT November 2024 (15 problems) + HMMT February 2025 (15 problems).

## Critique & known issues

- **30 problems is small.** A single problem swings ~3 points; 95 vs 97 is within noise.
- **Contamination risk** — problems are public on AoPS within hours; any post-tournament-trained model has plausibly seen them.
- **Thinking-mode dominance.** Reasoning-mode models score 90+; non-reasoning models often 30+ points lower. Always report mode.
- **Saturation underway.** With GPT-5.2 reportedly at 100% and most frontier > 92%, HMMT 2025 is following AIME 2025 into the saturated zone. HMMT 2026 problems (when released) will be the next refresh.
- **Math style is olympiad** — does not test research math (see [FrontierMath](FrontierMath.md)).
